# Go-To-Market Plan

## Exact Target User

**Engineering Manager at a Series A SaaS startup (15–60 people), 2–3 years in.**

Not "startups" broadly. Specifically: the person who gets the monthly Carta expense report, sees $3,000 in AI subscriptions, and thinks "I should figure out if we're spending this well" — but never has the time to actually audit it. They have budget authority but no dedicated FinOps function. They're on Hacker News, follow AI tool release threads, and have an opinion on Cursor vs Copilot.

Secondary: Indie hacker or technical founder running a 1–5 person shop who pays for multiple AI tools personally and is cost-conscious.

1. **Post on Hacker News** — "Show HN: SpendLens — free AI spend audit for engineering teams." Time it for Tuesday 9am EST. The tool is genuinely useful so it should get traction. Target: 50 users from one post.

2. **DM 20 engineering managers on LinkedIn** — Search "Engineering Manager" + "Series A" in SF/NYC. Message: "Built a free tool that audits AI tool spend in 60 seconds — took 2 min to make me feel stupid about my Claude plan. Mind trying it?" Personalized, no ask. Target: 10 users.

3. **Post in 3 Slack communities** — Indie Hackers, Lenny's Newsletter, Rands Leadership Slack. Short post with the link and a screenshot of a real audit showing $400/mo savings. Target: 20 users.

4. **Tweet the results of a real audit** — "Ran our AI tool stack through SpendLens. Found $340/mo in savings in 90 seconds. Here's what we're switching." Screenshot. Tag the tool vendors. Target: 20 users from retweets.

Total realistic: 100 users in week 1 if HN lands.
