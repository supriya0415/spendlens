# User Interviews

Three conversations with potential users — engineering managers and technical founders who pay for AI tools.

---

## Interview 1 — R.K., Engineering Manager, 22-person B2B SaaS startup

**Date:** 2025-05-10 | **Duration:** 12 minutes | **How:** College friend, now EM at a fintech startup

**Background:** Manages a team of 6 engineers. Uses Cursor, GitHub Copilot, and ChatGPT Team. Has an expense budget he reviews monthly.

**3+ Direct Quotes:**

> "I literally just renewed all three of these last week without thinking. I don't even know what I'm paying for half of them."

> "The thing is, GitHub Copilot and Cursor overlap almost completely for my team. I've been meaning to pick one for six months."

> "If something showed me the exact dollar difference and said 'switch this one,' I'd do it in ten minutes. I just don't want to research it myself."

**The most surprising thing he said:**

He didn't think of his AI subscriptions as "AI spend" — he thought of them as "software subscriptions." When I framed it as "AI spend audit" he said "oh I guess you're right, I'm spending like $400/mo on this category and have never actually looked at it as a category." The category framing was the unlock.

**What it changed about the design:**

Changed the hero copy from "AI Spend Audit" to "Stop guessing. Know what you're wasting on AI." — leads with the pain, not the category label. Also added the team context section to the form because he immediately asked "does it account for team size?"

## Interview 2 — P.S., Solo Founder / Indie Hacker

**Date:** 2025-05-10 | **Duration:** 10 minutes | **How:** Twitter DM, builds developer tools

**Background:** 1-person company. Pays for Claude Pro, ChatGPT Plus, and Cursor Pro personally — ~$60/mo. Very cost-conscious.

**3+ Direct Quotes:**

> "I pay for both ChatGPT and Claude because I use them for different things. But honestly I don't know if that's smart or if I could just use one."

> "The problem is every comparison blog post is written by someone who's never actually tried to replace one with the other."

> "I would 100% share something like this on Twitter if it told me I was wasting money. Especially if the share link showed the result — that's the kind of thing that gets quote-tweeted."

**The most surprising thing he said:**

He said the share URL was the most important feature, not the audit. "The audit is for me. The share link is what makes it spread." He specifically mentioned wanting to share it to show off that he's optimized, not just to share savings. Ego is a growth mechanism.

**What it changed about the design:**

Made the share card more prominent in the results page. Changed copy from "Share this audit" to "Share this audit — show off your optimized stack." Added context that identifying PII is stripped (he cared about privacy before sharing).

## Interview 3 — A.M., CTO, 8-person early-stage startup

**Date:** 2025-05-11 | **Duration:** 15 minutes | **How:** Met at a local startup meetup

**Background:** Technical co-founder, wears the EM hat too. 3 engineers. Pays for Cursor Business, Anthropic API (directly), and Claude Team. Spends ~$800/mo on AI infrastructure.

**3+ Direct Quotes:**

> "The Anthropic API bill is the one that surprises me. We just integrated it and the usage scaled faster than I expected."

> "I know we should probably audit this but it's never the most urgent thing. Something that does it automatically in 60 seconds is actually the right intervention."

> "I would book a call with Credex if the tool found real savings. If it's just 'switch from X to Y' I can do that myself. The value of Credex is the credits discount — make that the pitch, not the advice."

**The most surprising thing he said:**

He pushed back on the Credex CTA framing. He said "don't tell me Credex will help me save money — show me the savings first, then say Credex can capture them automatically." The advice/audit is the free thing. Credex is for people who don't want to implement the advice themselves.

**What it changed about the design:**

Rewrote the Credex CTA from "Credex can help you save" to "Credex can lock in these savings" — implies the savings are already identified (they are), and Credex is the execution mechanism. Also only shows the CTA for audits >$500/mo, which he suggested: "if I'm only saving $50, I don't need a consultant."
